Evaluation Methodology

This section outlines the foundational philosophy used to assess governance entities. It provides context on what benefits are prioritized, what constraints shape the challenges, and how risk is classified to protect MAISA.

🌟

Benefits: Trust over Standards

Prioritizes entities that foster semantic interoperability, maintain the "Lake-First" architecture, and support informed instruction without compromising privacy.

⚠️

Challenges: Operational Constraints

Evaluated against MAISA’s lack of a formal internal IT department, reliance on grant-based funding, and the risk of "hidden work" burdens on local ISD staff.

🛡️

Risk Isolation Framework

  • Low: MAISA is directly liable.
  • Moderate: Liability shared across consortium (e.g., JPA).
  • High: Dedicated legal subsidiary/managed service acts as a "firebreak" with its own liability.

    Compliance & Ambiguity Protocols

    This section outlines the strict data classification rules required for FERPA compliance and the established protocols for handling legal ambiguities regarding data ownership.

    🔒

    FERPA Classification

    Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

    Access restricted via Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC). Only "Legitimate Educational Interest" (LEI) warrants decryption or access. Enforced at the Amorphic layer.

    Directory Information

    Managed via "Opt-Out" protocols at the ISD level. In MiGDL, this is treated as "Protected" requiring role-based authorization, with lower encryption overhead for public reports.

    The "Bronze Layer" Rule

    No PII is removed or altered in the Bronze layer, maintaining an immutable audit trail. Redaction and de-identification occur strictly in the Silver/Gold transformation pipelines.

    ⚖️

    Ambiguity Protocol

    Flagged Item: Legal mechanism for "Ownership Transfer" from ISDs to the Data Trust remains at ~60% clarity.

    Alternative A

    Retain ISD ownership; Trust acts as "Contracted Agent" under FERPA School Official Exception.

    Alternative B

    Complete transfer of ownership to a 501(c)(3) subsidiary governed by the ISDs.

    ✅

    Recommendation: Alternative A

    Minimizes "consent" requirements and relies on established School Official Exception precedents, providing the most robust and immediate risk-mitigation path.
